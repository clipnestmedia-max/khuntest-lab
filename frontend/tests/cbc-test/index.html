What is the CBC test?

A CBC test measures important blood cells including red blood cells, white blood cells, haemoglobin and platelets. It is one of the most commonly ordered pathology tests.

Why is it done?

  • Screening for anaemia or infection.
  • Monitoring platelet count and blood cell changes.
  • Supporting fever, weakness, fatigue and routine health checkups.

Preparation

No fasting is usually required unless your doctor has advised it with another test.
